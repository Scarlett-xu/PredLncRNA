With the development of high-throughput sequencing technology, numerous genomic transcript data have been discovered, which promotes the study of genomics, especially the human genome. However, it is worth noting that >90% of the transcripts in the human genome are not encoded into proteins, well known as noncoding RNAs (ncRNAs). Long noncoding RNAs,(long ncRNAs or lncRNAs) are a class of ncRNAs with >200 nucleotides in length.
According to the regions that lncRNAs are located and transcribed from, lncRNAs can be divided into four major categories: intergenic lncRNAs (those transcribed from intergenic regions), intronic lncRNAs (those transcribed entirely from introns of protein-coding genes), sense lncRNAs (those transcribed from the sense strand of protein-coding genes) and antisense lncRNAs(those transcribed from the antisense strand of protein-coding genes). According to effects exerted on DNA sequences, lncRNAs can be classified into two classes: cis-lncRNAs that regulate the expression of genes in close genomic proximity and translncRNAs that regulate the expression of distant genes.
LncRNAs had been supposed to have no biological func- tions because they could not encode proteins. However, recent advances found that lncRNAs perform important functions in various biological processes. For instance, lncRNAs participate in transcription, splicing, translation, protein localization, cellular structure integrity, imprinting, cell cycle and apopto- sis, stem cell pluripotency and reprogramming, and heat shock response. Some of the abnormally expressed lncRNAs are widely distributed in tumor tissue, involving tumors of various systems. For example, an lncRNA named the Transcribed-Ultra Conserved Regions is found to have dramatically changed based expression levels in both pediatric neuroblastoma and chronic lymphocytic leukemia of adults. Some lncRNAs can lead to overgrowth of cells by interfering with apoptosis, and thus cause tumors.
LncRNA prediction is the prerequisite for studying and under- standing biogenesis and functions of lncRNAs. However, dis- criminating lncRNAs from protein-coding RNAs is challenging because lncRNAs share many features similar to protein-coding RNAs. Moreover, in vivo experiments for lncRNA prediction are usually time consuming and expensive.
In the last decade, the researchers collected lncRNAs from RefSeq, Ensembl Genomes, GENCODE, etc., and built several lncRNA datasets. These databases not only provide coding and noncoding transcripts, but also provide extended genomic information, which is likewise pragmatic in the field of lncRNA prediction and make it possible to develop the computational methods and help the further investigation.

To facilitate the lncRNA prediction, the researchers consider many features that can differentiate the coding and noncoding sequences, and these features are classified into six classes: codon-related features, open reading frame (ORF)-related fea- tures, guanine–cytosine (GC)-related features, coding sequence- related features, transcript-related features and structure- related features. In this light, many computational methods have been developed for predicting lncRNAs, and they utilize different features and different machine learning techniques to build prediction models. These computational methods are classified into three classes:

  • Binary classifier-based methods: This category of methods utilizes traditional machine learning classifiers to identify lncRNAs. The researchers used statistical features of lncR- NAs and used classifiers such as support vector machine (SVM), random forest (RF) and logic regression (LR) to build the prediction models.
  • Deep learning-based methods: This category of methods make use of deep learning techniques to identify lncRNAs. Deep learning is an advanced and popular class of machine learning techniques, demonstrating outstanding performance in computer vision and natural language processing . Deep learning can help to automatically extract features from lncRNAs and boost the lncRNA prediction, and thus several deep learning-based lncRNA prediction methods, such as LncADeep and lncRNAnet , have been developed.
  • Ensemble learning-based methods: This category of methods adopt ensemble learning techniques to build prediction models and identify lncRNAs. Ensemble learning is a class of strategies that combine several submodels to construct an overall prediction model. It is noteworthy that ensemble learning also can be used to integrate different features or datasets. Ensemble learning is proved to improve the generalization performance of the lncRNA prediction. For example, PredLnc-GFStack integrates different features and uses them to train various classifiers, taking the average score of all classifiers as the final prediction.
